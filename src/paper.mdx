**RoboProcessBench** evaluates whether VLMs understand *how* robotic manipulation unfolds, not only whether it succeeds.

A process-aware benchmark and supervision corpus with 12 diagnostic task families, ~58k QA items, 260 manipulation tasks, and SFT-trained VLM process evaluators.

## Why process-aware?

<div class="not-prose grid grid-cols-1 gap-6 md:grid-cols-2">
  <div class="rounded-xl border border-border bg-card p-5">

##### Outcome-only evaluation

**Did the task succeed?** Only the final success state is considered.

  </div>
  <div class="rounded-xl border border-border bg-card p-5">

##### Process-aware evaluation

<ul>
  <li>Has contact been established?</li>
  <li>Is the robot still moving?</li>
  <li>Is the primitive early, middle, or late?</li>
  <li>Are frames temporally consistent?</li>
  <li>What primitive should happen next?</li>
</ul>

  </div>
</div>

## Results

### Key findings

Current VLMs are fragmented: they can recognize some local states, but struggle with primitive-local progress and temporal reasoning.

<div class="not-prose space-y-3">
  <FindingBlock number={1} title="Zero-shot strengths are fragmented. No single VLM excels across all 12 tasks; strengths cluster by task family." />
  <FindingBlock number={2} title="Local process states are easier than local progress. VLMs recognize static states (contact, phase) but fail on within-primitive progress estimation (T5)." />
  <FindingBlock number={3} title="Temporal reconstruction remains near chance. Temporal ordering (T8) and temporal priority (T9) are the hardest tasks across all models." />
</div>

## ProcessData-SFT: from benchmark to trainable supervision

Beyond benchmarking, ProcessData enables supervised fine-tuning to build dedicated process evaluators.

<div class="not-prose text-center text-sm text-muted-foreground space-y-1 italic">
  <div>Base VLM (Qwen2.5-VL-7B / InternVL-3-8B)</div>
